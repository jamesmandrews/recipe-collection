# Orange Chicken Rice Bowls

> Crispy pan-fried chicken tossed in a homemade orange sauce, served over bone broth rice. Better than Panda Express.

## Ingredients

### Chicken

| Ingredient | Imperial | Metric |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Chicken breast | 2.5 lbs | 1.13 kg |
| Corn starch | 3 tbsp | 40 g |
| Salt | to taste | to taste |
| Black pepper | to taste | to taste |
| Paprika | to taste | to taste |

### Sauce

| Ingredient | Imperial | Metric |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Light orange juice | 1 cup + 2 tbsp | 275 g |
| Soy sauce | 3 1/2 tbsp | 50 g |
| Honey | 1 1/2 tbsp | 30 g |
| Corn starch | 4 tsp | 20 g |
| Minced garlic | 1 tbsp | 14 g |

### Rice

| Ingredient | Imperial | Metric |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Cooked rice | 4 cups | 4 cups |
| Bone broth (used to cook the rice) | 1.5 cups total | 360 ml |

## Instructions

1. Cube the chicken breast. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika, then add 40 g corn starch and mix until coated.
2. Pan fry the chicken until cooked through, about 4–5 minutes per side.
3. While the chicken is cooking, blend the sauce: combine orange juice, soy sauce, honey, garlic, and 20 g corn starch in a blender until smooth.
4. Pour the sauce into a pan over low heat and stir until it thickens.
5. Once the sauce is thick, add the chicken back in and toss to coat.
6. Divide the rice across 4 bowls (1 cup each) and top with the orange chicken.

## Notes

- Cook rice with bone broth instead of water for extra flavor and protein.
- Pre-cook a larger batch of rice to make meal prep easier.
- The sauce thickens quickly on low heat — keep an eye on it so it doesn't get too thick.
