# Ground Chicken Ranchero Wraps

> A freezer-friendly wrap inspired by Chicken Ranchero, using ground chicken and a thick, spiced tomato filling. Make a batch, freeze individually, and reheat for a quick meal.

## Details

| | |
|-----|-----|
| **Cuisine** | Mexican |
| **Prep Time** | 10 minutes |
| **Cook Time** | 20 minutes |
| **Total Time** | 30 minutes |
| **Servings** | 8 wraps |

## Ingredients

### Filling
| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Avocado oil | 1 tbsp (15ml) |
| Ground chicken | 1.5 lb (680g) |
| Yellow onion, small dice | 1 |
| Bell pepper, small dice | 1 |
| Fresh jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional) | 1 |
| Garlic, finely minced | 4 cloves |
| Cumin | ½ tsp (1g) |
| Paprika | ½ tsp (1g) |
| Salt | ½ tsp (3g) |
| Pepper | ¼ tsp (0.5g) |
| Granulated garlic | ¼ tsp (0.5g) |
| Chicken stock | ¼ cup (60ml) |
| Crushed tomatoes | ½ can (7 oz / 200g) |

### Assembly
| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Large flour tortillas | 8 |
| Shredded Monterey Jack or Queso Fresco | 1 cup (115g) |

### Fresh Garnish (add when serving, not before freezing)
| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Avocado | as desired |
| Sour cream | as desired |
| Fresh cilantro, chopped | as desired |
| Lime wedges | as desired |

## Instructions

1. Heat avocado oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground chicken, breaking it into small pieces with a spatula. Season with salt, pepper, and granulated garlic. Cook for 5-6 minutes until browned and cooked through. Remove chicken from pan and set aside.
2. In the same pan, add onion, bell pepper, and jalapeño (if using). Sauté over medium-high heat for 5-7 minutes until softened. Add minced garlic, cumin, and paprika and cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
3. Return the chicken to the pan. Add chicken stock and crushed tomatoes, stirring to combine. Simmer over medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is thick and not saucy. You want a dry, scoopable filling. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed.
4. Let the filling cool completely before assembling wraps.
5. Spoon filling down the center of each tortilla, sprinkle with cheese, and roll up tightly, tucking in the ends.

## Freezing and Reheating

- Wrap each assembled wrap individually in foil or parchment paper, then place in a freezer bag. Freeze for up to 3 months.
- **Microwave:** Remove foil, wrap in a damp paper towel, and microwave for 2-3 minutes, flipping halfway.
- **Oven:** Keep wrapped in foil and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15-20 minutes from frozen.
- Add fresh garnishes (avocado, sour cream, cilantro, lime) after reheating.

## Notes

- The key difference from the original Chicken Ranchero is less liquid — the filling needs to be thick so the wraps don't get soggy, especially after freezing.
- For more heat, leave the jalapeño seeds in or add a pinch of cayenne.
- You can swap flour tortillas for whole wheat or low-carb wraps.
