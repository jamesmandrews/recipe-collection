# Korean Galbi (LA Kalbi Short Ribs)

> Sweet and savory marinated beef short ribs grilled to caramelized perfection — a Korean BBQ classic.

## Details

| | |
|-----|-----|
| **Source** | Traditional Korean recipe |
| **Cuisine** | Korean |
| **Servings** | 4-6 |
| **Prep Time** | 20 minutes + 4-24 hours marinating |
| **Cook Time** | 6-10 minutes |
| **Storage** | Fridge 3-4 days • Freeze 3 months (marinated, uncooked) |

## Ingredients

### Short Ribs
| Ingredient | Imperial | Metric |
|------------|----------|--------|
| LA-style (flanken-cut) beef short ribs, cut ¼ inch thick across the bone | 4 lb | 1.8 kg |

### Marinade
| Ingredient | Imperial | Metric |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Soy sauce | ½ cup | 120 ml |
| Toasted sesame oil | 3 tbsp | 45 ml |
| Brown sugar | 3 tbsp | 40 g |
| Mirin (rice wine) | 2 tbsp | 30 ml |
| Rice vinegar | 1 tbsp | 15 ml |
| Asian pear, grated (see notes) | ½ pear | ½ cup / 120 g |
| Yellow onion, grated | ½ medium | ¼ cup / 40 g |
| Garlic, minced | 5 cloves | 1½ tbsp / 15 g |
| Fresh ginger, grated (1-inch piece) | 1 tbsp | 6 g |
| Water | 2 tbsp | 30 ml |
| Black pepper, freshly ground | ½ tsp | 1 g |

### Garnish
| Ingredient | Imperial | Metric |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Green onions (scallions), thinly sliced | 2 | 2 |
| Toasted sesame seeds | 1 tbsp | 9 g |

## Instructions

### Prepare the Ribs
1. Rinse the short ribs under cold water. Place in a large bowl of cold water and soak for 30 minutes to draw out excess blood. Drain, rinse again, and pat dry with paper towels.

### Make the Marinade
1. Combine soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, mirin, rice vinegar, grated Asian pear, grated onion, minced garlic, grated ginger, water, and black pepper in a medium bowl. Whisk until the sugar is dissolved.

### Marinate
1. Place the ribs in a large zip-lock bag or shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the ribs, making sure each piece is well coated.
2. Seal and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours, ideally overnight (up to 24 hours). Turn the bag or flip the ribs once halfway through.

### Grill
1. Preheat a charcoal or gas grill to high heat (450-500°F / 230-260°C). Clean and oil the grates well — the sugar in the marinade will stick if grates are not properly oiled.
2. Remove ribs from the marinade, letting excess drip off. Place on the grill in a single layer.
3. Grill for 3-4 minutes per side, until nicely charred and caramelized. The thin cut cooks quickly — do not overcook or the meat will become tough.

### Serve
1. Transfer ribs to a cutting board and let rest for 2-3 minutes.
2. Arrange on a platter and garnish with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds.

## Serving Suggestions

- Steamed white rice
- Lettuce leaves for wrapping (butter lettuce or perilla leaves)
- Ssamjang (Korean dipping paste)
- Kimchi
- Quick-pickled radish (danmuji)

## Notes

- **The Asian pear is key.** It acts as a natural tenderizer due to its enzymes and adds subtle sweetness. If unavailable, substitute ½ Bosc pear plus 1 tbsp (15 ml) apple juice.
- **Flanken cut matters.** Ask your butcher for short ribs cut across the bone, about ¼ inch (6 mm) thick. Korean grocery stores almost always carry this cut.
- **Don't marinate longer than 24 hours.** The pear enzymes can make the meat mushy.
- **No grill?** Use a cast-iron grill pan or broiler set to high, about 4 inches (10 cm) from the heat source, 3-4 minutes per side.
