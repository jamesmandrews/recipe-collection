# Pizza Gyoza

> Crispy pan-fried gyoza with a molten cheesy Italian-style filling — like a pizza pocket meets a dumpling.

## Details

| | |
|-----|-----|
| **Cuisine** | Japanese-Italian Fusion |
| **Servings** | 4 (24 gyoza) |
| **Frying Temp** | Medium (around 175°C / 350°F) |

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Imperial | Metric |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Gyoza wrappers | 24 | 24 |
| Ricotta cheese | 1 cup | 250g |
| Low-moisture shredded mozzarella | ½ cup | 60g |
| Tomato paste or thick tomato sauce | 2 tbsp | 30g |
| Parmesan, grated | 2 tbsp | 15g |
| Garlic cloves, minced | 2 | 2 |
| Dried oregano | 1 tsp | 1 tsp |
| Dried basil | 1 tsp | 1 tsp |
| Chili flakes | ¼ tsp | ¼ tsp |
| Salt and pepper | pinch | pinch |
| Egg, beaten (for sealing) | 1 | 1 |
| Oil for pan frying | ¼ cup | 60ml |

## Instructions

1. **Make the filling:** Combine the ricotta, mozzarella, tomato paste, parmesan, garlic, oregano, basil, chili flakes, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Mix well until fully combined. Taste and adjust seasoning.
2. **Rest the filling:** Cover and refrigerate for 15 minutes. This firms it up and makes it much easier to work with.
3. **Fill and seal:** Place a gyoza wrapper in your palm. Add about 1 teaspoon of filling to the center — don't overfill. Brush the edges with beaten egg, fold in half, and pinch firmly to seal. Double-pinch the edges to make sure there are no gaps. Repeat with remaining wrappers.
4. **Heat the pan:** Heat the oil in a shallow non-stick or cast-iron pan over medium heat until shimmering.
5. **Pan fry:** Place the gyoza in the pan in a single layer, flat side down. Cook for 2–3 minutes until the bottoms are golden brown. Add ¼ cup (60ml) of water to the pan and immediately cover with a lid. Steam for 2–3 minutes until the wrappers are translucent and the filling is melted. Remove the lid and cook for another minute to crisp the bottoms back up.
6. **Serve:** Let them rest for a minute before serving — the filling will be very hot. Serve with a simple marinara or garlic butter sauce on the side for dipping.

## Notes

- Double-pinch the edges well to prevent any leaks during frying.
- Don't overfill — about 1 teaspoon per wrapper is plenty.
